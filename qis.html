Quantum Computational Science

Quantum computers promise new computational paradigms as hardware limitations decrease. Their superiority is justified rather succintly with the following statement: an operational quantum computer would immediatley render obsolete some forms of cryptography currently implemented today (namely Shor's Algorithim and it's application to the widely used RSA encryption scheme).

Basic Quantum Algorithims

Many algorithims use more basic and smaller algorithims as components. Arguably, the three most general and basic quantum algorithims are the Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT), the quantum algorithim for phase estimation of some unitary operatory (Phase Estimation), and the quantum algorithim for the amplification of some desirable subspace in some larger superposition (Amplitude Amplification). These three algorithims build off of each other in the order just espoused, that is, the QFT is the simplest, while phase estimation incorporates the QFT, and amplitude amplification generally requires the use of phase estimation.

Qiskit

Qiskit is a python module that allows users to explicitly code quantum circuit frameworks on which simulations can be run. It also allows users to run quantum experiments on real quantum computers at IBM, though all the limitations of modern technology apply (low qubit counts, poor fidelity gates, and non-negligible noise).

Resources

The classic reference textbook for Quantum Information and Computational Science is the fantastic Quantum Computation and Quantum Information by Michael A. Nielsen and Isaac L. Chuang. This text is also a reasonably good introduction to the principles of QIS. However, a separate education in linear algebra would be strongly recommended if it is to be taken as a starting point. It can be purchased in print (although it can be found relatively easily online in pdf form due to it's ubiquity).

For those first getting into the world of quantum programming with QISkit, a wonderful free online textbook written as an introduction to both QIS and Qiskit can be found at Qiskit textbook. It's written in a mixture of html and interactive jupyter notebook sections with real code, allowing for a more interactive experience.
