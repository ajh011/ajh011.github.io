The Laplace Equation

The Laplace equation is a differential equation equivalent to the statement that a function, evaluated at any point, is the average of all of it's surrounding points. This also specifies that a function doesn't have any extremal points in a region.

General Solutions

By the method of separation of variables the Laplace equation may be solved in general for most coordinate systems.

Cylindrical General Solution

The general solution takes the form \(\psi(\rho,\phi,z)\), with the separated solutions in various functional bases described below.

Radial

$$ \begin{align} R(\rho)=A J_m(k\rho)+B N_m(k\rho) \end{align} $$

Polar

$$ \begin{align} Q(\phi)&=A\sin(m\phi)+B\cos(m\phi)\\ Q(\phi)&=Ce^{im\phi}+De^{-im\phi} \end{align} $$

Cartesian

$$ \begin{align} \mathcal{Z}(z)&=A\sinh(kz)+B\cosh(kz)\\ \mathcal{Z}(z)&=Ce^{kz}+De^{-kz} \end{align} $$
