Resources for Quantum Computation and Quantum Information

Below is a list of resources for those interested in quantum computational and information science. Generally ordered by media type, some resources have additional medias and problem sets associated with them.

Most assume a familiarity with basic linear algebra and eignedecomposition. Thus, background knowledge in such mathematics is strongly recommended if not required. The unitary group is also of particular importance for these fields.


Textbooks

Many good textbooks are now available for those interested in these fields, of varying assumed background and difficulty. Hence, relevant texts are broken down below into two subjective groups, reference texts and introductory texts.

Introductory Texts

Several introductory texts are now available. One includes N. David Mermin's Quantum Computer Science: An Introduction.

Reference Texts

The classic reference text, at this point, for the field is Nielsen and Chuang's Quantum Computation and Quantum Information.


Online Lectures

John Preskill's lecture series, which can be found on youtube here: Preskill Lectures. Accompanying this wonderful lecture set is a full set of notes by the lecturer as well as problem sets available on the course website.
