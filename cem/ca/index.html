Clifford Algebras

We all know areas and volumes can be given a scalar magnitude. Clifford alegbras allow us to extend this magnitude and encode some orientation along with this magnitude much as we do for oriented lengths as vectors. Effectively it's the mathematical tool set that allows us to work with oriented n-dimensional slices of some space. It introduces many concepts sometimes with several names for similar objects, so for those confused by these matters there is a small section here discussing, at large, these nuances and synonyms.
The exterior product is also known as the wedge product and is an effective generalization of the cross product sometimes encountered. Furthermore, the exterior product (which we shall henceforth call the wedge product) defines an exterior algebra over some vector space. These exterior algebras are also known as the Grassman algebras, but they only include specifically sums of k-blades. Clifford algebras then allow us to define sums of arbitrary order blades (i.e. sums of blades not neccesarily of the same order), known as multivectors; this is accomplished with the introduction of a new product called the geometric product. Blades

Vector Products

For ease of transition, we first define two products that can be combined to form the geometric product (however, both can be defined in terms only of the geometric product, as is shown later). The first is the familiar dot product, which most physicists are familiar with; the second is an antisymmetric product know as the wedge product (or exterior product), which may not be encountered in a course of undergraduate classes. The wedge product is related to the cross product but only as a special case for three dimensions, which allows for an isomorphism between areas and spatial directions.

Dot Product

The dot product is a multi-linear symmetric product that given two blades of order \(k\) and \(l\) returns a blade of \(k-l\). It is something of a measure of orthogonality, or mutual projection, in that two blades of the same dimension return zero if all the basis elements are orthogonal.

Wedge Product

The wedge product is a multi-linear antisymmetric product that given two blades of order \(k\) and \(l\) returns a blade of \(k+l\). It is something of a measure of mutual rejection, in that two blades of the same dimension return zero if all the basis elements are parallel.
